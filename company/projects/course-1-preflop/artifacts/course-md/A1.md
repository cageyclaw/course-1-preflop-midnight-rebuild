# A1 — RFI (Raise First In)

## Intro
RFI (Raise First In) is the **first raise when action folds to you**. It’s the baseline range that quietly controls everything that happens later: how often you steal blinds, how often you face 3-bets, and what kinds of postflop spots you land in.

This course uses preflop charts as **structure, not a prison**. Your job is to start with a baseline, then adjust for position, stack depth, and opponents.

> Reference: PokerCoaching — Preflop Charts (position-by-position opens + sizing heuristics)
> https://pokercoaching.com/preflop-charts

---

## Key rules
### 1) Position is the main dial
- **Earlier position (EP) = tighter** opens. You’ll be out of position more often and face stronger ranges behind.
- **Later position (LP) = wider** opens because you act later postflop and win more pots uncontested.

PokerCoaching frames position as one of the two biggest levers in preflop strategy (along with stack depth).
- https://pokercoaching.com/blog/preflop-strategy-guide-mastering-position-and-stack-depth/

### 2) Use charts to learn the “bottom of range”
Instead of trying to memorize every combo, learn the **cutoff hands** (the weakest opens) per position. If you know the bottom of the range, you can reconstruct the rest faster.
- https://pokercoaching.com/blog/how-to-study-preflop-ranges-and-poker-strategies/

### 3) Default open size: keep it consistent
A simple baseline is **2.5x** when opening. Consistency reduces sizing tells (big = strong, small = weak).
- https://pokercoaching.com/preflop-charts

## Examples
### Example 1 — Position changes everything
You’re dealt **K9s**:
- UTG: often a **fold** (domination risk + poor realization).
- CO/BTN: often an **open** (position lets you realize equity and pressure blinds).

### Example 2 — Steal opportunity
Blinds are tight and over-folding. Your BTN opens can become **highly profitable** because you win more pots uncontested. (This depends on opponents; it’s not guaranteed.)

### Example 3 — When not to push thin opens
If blinds are loose/sticky and you get called a lot, tighten your weakest opens (especially offsuit junk) and value-open a bit more.

---

## Common mistakes
- **Opening the same range from every position.** That’s a leak disguised as “confidence.”
- **Changing open size based on hand strength.** Congratulations, you invented a tell.
- **Over-opening in early position** because “it’s suited.” Suit helps, but EP still matters.
- **Not adjusting to the blinds.** If they defend a ton, stop giving them cheap equity with marginal opens.

## Drills / checklists
### 10-minute RFI drill (daily)
1. Pick **one position** (start with BTN).
2. Pick **one stack depth** (default: **100bb**; or choose your most common depth).
3. Run 50–100 “folded to you” spots and track:
   - Did you open/fold correctly?
   - Which hands are at the bottom of your range?

### RFI decision checklist
- What position am I in?
- Who is in the blinds (tight folders vs sticky defenders vs 3-bettors)?
- Am I using my default sizing (e.g., 2.5x)?
- Is my hand near the **bottom of range** (be extra disciplined)?

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
Even perfect RFIs can lose short-term. You’ll open the right hand, get 3-bet, fold, and feel like you’re “bleeding.” That’s normal. Preflop edges are often **small but compounding**—judge decisions by long-run EV and accuracy, not by whether this orbit worked out.
