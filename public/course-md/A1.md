# RFI Fundamentals

## Objective
Build a repeatable **raise‑first‑in (RFI)** baseline: tight early, wide late, and consistent sizing so your preflop tree is clean and fast.

## Definitions
- **RFI (Raise First In):** You open the pot with a raise after everyone folds to you.
- **Bottom of range:** The weakest hands you still open in a position.
- **Open size:** Your standard raise size (keep it consistent).

## Rules
1. **Position is the main dial.** Early position = tight; Button = widest.
2. **Use one open size** for the entire range (default: **2.5x**).
3. **Protect your bottom of range.** If you hate facing a 3‑bet with a hand, it’s probably not an open yet.
4. **SB is not the Button.** You’re OOP forever; tighten.

![Position widening ladder](/course-md/position-ladder.svg)

## Exceptions
- **Aggressive 3‑bettors behind:** tighten your CO/HJ bottom range.
- **Blinds over‑folding:** widen BTN steals (still same size).
- **Short stacks (<40bb):** reduce speculative opens; prioritize high‑card value.
