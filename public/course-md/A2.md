# Opening Ranges and Position

## Objective
Learn how **position shapes your opening ranges** so you stop leaking chips from early seats and print more from late seats.

## Definitions
- **Position:** Where you act relative to the dealer (UTG → BTN → blinds).
- **Range width:** How many hands you open from a seat.
- **Steal:** Late‑position open aimed at winning the blinds.

## Rules
1. **Tight early, wide late.** UTG/HJ are disciplined; CO/BTN open wider.
2. **BTN is your profit seat.** Most steals come from here.
3. **SB is a trap.** Open tighter than BTN because you’ll be out of position.
4. **Consistency beats creativity.** Use your baseline chart before “mixing it up.”

![RFI range bands by position](/course-md/rfi-range-bands.svg)

## Exceptions
- **Blinds defend too wide:** tighten your weakest steals.
- **Blinds over‑fold:** widen your Button opens (same size).
- **Tough 3‑bettor on your left:** tighten the seat before them.
