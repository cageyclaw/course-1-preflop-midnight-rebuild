# Short Stack Play

## Objective
Adjust your ranges and sizing when stacks are **40bb or less** so you stop bleeding in shallow‑stack spots.

## Definitions
- **Effective stack:** The smallest stack involved in the hand.
- **Jam:** All‑in preflop shove.
- **Equity realization:** How well a hand converts equity into value postflop.

## Rules
1. **Open smaller.** 2.0–2.2x is standard when shallow.
2. **Prioritize high‑card value.** Trash suited connectors lose power.
3. **3‑bet jam more linearly.** Shallow stacks reduce postflop skill edge.
4. **Avoid speculative flats.** Low SPR removes implied odds.

![Stack depth scale](/course-md/stack-depth-scale.svg)

## Exceptions
- **Loose callers:** widen value opens and punish with bigger sizes.
- **Nitty tables:** steal more from late position.
- **ICM pressure:** tighten all‑in bluffs, especially as a mid stack.
